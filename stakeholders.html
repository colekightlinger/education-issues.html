Stakeholders

Stakeholding groups: The UN. Department of International Development.
Perspectives on issue:The UN's perspective:In 2000 164 countries agreed to make education available for all. They have been
trying to solve the problem of education since then. They are affected by the loss of education for their children and the
next generation. The DFID's perspective: They wish to get an equal amount of girls into school as boys. They wish to solve
the issue so their will be less gender inequality.
Addressing the issue:The UN's address on education: They have increased primary school enrollment worldwide and the number
of out of school children decreased by about 13 million between 1999 and 2009. Positive affects of this could stop so many
young people from dropping out of school early. Negative affects could be the amount of money spent to improve education.
It could make several countries go broke. The DFID's address on education: They plan to improve education and the
availability of education in 25 or more countries around the world.
